\textbf{Background.}
This codebook documents all variables, data sources, derived measures, and
analytical indices used in a Bayesian spatio-temporal joint disease modelling
study of HIV and Tuberculosis (TB) co-morbidity across Kenya's 47 counties
from 2012 to 2017.

\vspace{4pt}
\textbf{Data description.}
Two CSV files (47 counties $\times$ 16 columns each) contain annual case
notification counts and projected populations for HIV and TB. The analytical
pipeline computes Standardised Incidence Ratios (SIRs) via indirect
standardisation, fits a shared-component Poisson model in R-INLA, and
extracts spatial, temporal, and space-time interaction effects.

\vspace{4pt}
\textbf{Reuse potential.}
The data and code support reproduction of all figures and tables in the
companion publication, and can be extended to other disease pairs or
sub-national administrative units in Africa.
\end{mdframed}

population over the six study years, computed as
\(\bar{P}_i = P_{i,\mathrm{Total}} / 6\). It is used as the reference

\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsection{Standardised Incidence Ratios}

The SIR compares observed to expected counts:
\[
\mathrm{SIR}_{iy} = \frac{O_{iy}}{E_{iy}}
\]
SIR $> 1$ indicates above-national-average burden; SIR $< 1$ is below average.

\begin{tabular}{@{}L{2.4cm} L{3.0cm} L{4.0cm}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Variable} & \textbf{Formula} & \textbf{Description} \\
\midrule
\code{adj.HIV[,y]}   & \code{HIV\_y / expected.HIV[,y]} & County SIR for HIV, year $y$; 47$\times$6 matrix. \\[3pt]
\code{adj.HIV.total} & \code{HIV\_Total / expected.HIV\_Total} & Pooled HIV SIR. \\[3pt]
\code{adj.TB[,y]}    & \code{TB\_y / expected.TB[,y]} & County SIR for TB, year $y$. \\[3pt]
\code{adj.TB.total}  & \code{TB\_Total / expected.TB\_Total} & Pooled TB SIR. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsection{SIR Categories for Choropleth Mapping}

Continuous SIRs are discretised into four ordered categories using consistent
breakpoints across all years and diseases:
\(c = (0,\; 0.40,\; 0.70,\; 0.90,\; 6)\).

\begin{tabular}{@{}L{2.0cm} L{2.0cm} L{5.4cm}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Label} & \textbf{SIR range} & \textbf{Interpretation} \\
\midrule
\texttt{0.00--0.40} & $[0,\,0.40]$  & Very low: county rate $<40\%$ of national average. \\[3pt]
\texttt{0.41--0.70} & $(0.40,\,0.70]$ & Low burden. \\[3pt]
\texttt{0.71--0.99} & $(0.70,\,0.90]$ & Moderate: approaching national average. \\[3pt]
\texttt{1.00+}      & $> 0.90$        & High: at or above national average. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

% ── SECTION 9: NOTES ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Data Notes and Caveats}

\textbf{Row ordering.} CSV files are sorted alphabetically by county name, not
by \code{County\_ID}. The R script uses \code{match()} on the shapefile's
\texttt{COUNTY} field to reorder rows before merging.

\textbf{Column name discrepancy.} The HIV dataset uses \code{Sn.no} as the
county identifier; the TB dataset uses \code{County\_ID}. Both represent
the same codes (1--47) and are interchangeable in joins.

\textbf{Pooled denominator.} \code{PP\_Total} is the sum of six annual
populations (total person-years), not a single-year estimate. Use with
caution when computing point-in-time rates.

\textbf{INLA indexing.} All indices must be positive integers starting at 1.
The temporal index maps $\text{tm.idx} = \text{Year} - 2011$ so that
$2012 \mapsto 1$ and $2017 \mapsto 6$.

\textbf{Copy betas.} A copy-effect $\beta > 0$ indicates positive spatial or
temporal association between diseases; $\beta$ close to 0 indicates little
shared pattern. Range is constrained to $(0, \infty)$ in the prior.
